Why Do Verrucae Develop and Persist?

Viral verrucae aren't just random skin growths. They're caused by a persistent viral infection with specific characteristics:

  • HPV infection - Human Papilloma Virus (HPV) causes verrucae, with approximately 100 different types affecting various body areas

  • Immune system variations - Some people are naturally more resistant while others are more susceptible to HPV infections

  • Entry through skin breaks - The virus penetrates through small cracks, cuts, or breaks in your skin

  • Preferred environments - HPV thrives in warm, moist areas where skin is thick, like hands and feet

  • Contagious nature - Easy transmission through direct contact or contaminated surfaces

  • Deep root structure - Verrucae develop deep roots that make surface treatments ineffective

  • Viral persistence - The virus can remain dormant and reactivate, causing recurring outbreaks

HPV creates skin growths that often develop a cauliflower-like appearance and can spread to multiple areas of your body, including hands, feet, arms, neck, and face. While typically benign and painless, their visible presence can significantly impact your self-confidence and daily activities.

As dermatologist Dr. Adam Friedman explains, "Verrucae are among the most persistent skin conditions because they're caused by a virus that embeds deeply in skin tissue. Effective treatment requires targeting the viral source, not just the surface appearance."
